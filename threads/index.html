Everything that we learn here in terms of Orderflow can essentially be visualized in a Simple candlestick chart if we develop proper observation skills. Let's talk about each topic needed one by one. Market structure: Market Structure is simply a way to organize the price action into a set framework by analyzing behavior, condition and current flow of the market. This allows us to highlight basic behavioral patterns, such as trend, momentum, exhaustion or reversals in a very simplified manner. The most basic form of analysis is to identify our most important points and highlight them to ignore the noise that might occur in the middle. We do this by identifying higher highs, higher lows, lower highs and lower lows, within these four basic structures we can identify further price pattern continuation. The same way that in drawing, where we first prioritize sketching and outlining the most important features and then detailing becomes significantly easier. In order to reduce the noise of our chart, we first begin with identifying the Swing Lows and Swing Highs.
A Swing low represents a relative low point in price action within a given time frame, and a Swing High represents a relative high point in price action within a given time frame.
They are used to determine trends. In this particular case: A series of Swing lows and swing highs that are all rising indicates an upward trending movement.

Supply and Demand: The law of Supply and Demand is a theory that seeks to explain the relationship between the availability and desire for a product, and its price. Typically, low availability and high demand boost the price of an asset and high availability and low demand reduce its price. Using an example, If i had a business where i sell a particular product, and i don't have competition selling the same product as i do and a lot of people desire it, it will push the price higher. On the other hand, if I sell a product that can be found broadly in the market, and there aren't a lot of people interested in it, the price will drop. Summarizing:
  • When demand exceeds supply, prices are going higher
  • When supply exceeds demand, prices are going lower.
 Markets are no different at all, they constantly move due to supply and demand dynamics. Auction Market Theory and Volume Distribution The purpose of an auction is to facilitate trade, Financial Markets are said to be a Two-Way Auction process because price can range from low to high and from high to low- to distribute fairly the bids and offers of the market participants. It is also considered an auction because it will continue upwards until the last bidder steps in, and when the market is unable to find an incremental buyer at the highest possible price the auction is said to be completed. When the market starts auctioning too high, the participation will slow down and price begins to drive in the opposite direction, this happens because in order to attract new buyers we need to offer them more attractive prices.

One of our major goals as traders is to avoid overcrowded trades, Using common sense if we decide to take action in the place where most of the participants are agreeing to trade there.. Well.. maybe we don't really have any significant available information compared to the rest of the participants. I always like to use two analogies to define Market Behavior. Now let's dive into Topic 3. Liquidity, what is Liquidity? Liquidity is the availability of the other side to trade with. When we trade we are either taking or providing liquidity, when the price stabilizes (balance area) or does not manage to move in a fluid manner it's because Takers are not consuming enough liquidity and viceversa when the price manages to do it. Limit orders act as advertised liquidity whereas market executions are completed trades that have removed that liquidity. Passive flow (Limits) tend to operate with a heavier hand, these limit orders typically determine market structure as aggressive positioning gets absorbed at key points. Why Limit orders tend to be the heavier hand? When you execute a Market order, you are crossing the spread and you‚Äôre immediately in an unrealized loss. What is the Spread? A spread is the difference between the buy (advertised offers) and sells (advertised bids) quoted for an asset. Market Makers will provide liquidity in the form of a spread. This means that the price to instantly buy an asset will always be slightly higher than the underlying market, while the price to instantly sell will always be slightly below it.

Let's assume for a second that we see an asset that is currently trading at 10.00 and the stars represent one contract each, if we wanted to INSTANTLY buy, there is no quote at 10.00, because if that was the case the Market Maker couldn't make any money, so the liquidity they advertise is slightly above to capture this slight difference. So he places four contracts at 10.01 If we decided to buy three contracts, we would get filled at 10.01, but what would happen if we wanted to buy more, 15 for example? We would have to cross the spread until we find the price level where there's enough orders to complete our transaction, so we would end moving the price to 10.03 because only there are enough contracts to fill our buy.

Above Swing Highs and Below Swing Lows, this is because most of the people that base their trade in Technical Analysis, repeat similar behaviors in their terms of Invalidating a trade, so those are places that usually stop a lot of people and price reverse from there.
